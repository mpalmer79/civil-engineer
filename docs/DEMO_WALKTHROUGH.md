# Demo Walkthrough

This walkthrough helps a first-time visitor, recruiter, or technical evaluator
understand Civil Engineer AI in a few minutes without a verbal explanation.

Live demo: https://civil-engineer.up.railway.app/

## Purpose

Civil Engineer AI is a document-first, evidence-first, reviewer-controlled
stormwater review-support platform for municipal and civil engineering plan
review. The demo shows how a reviewer turns a submission into organized,
traceable, review-support evidence and a reviewer communication record, while a
human reviewer stays in control.

Brookside Meadows is a synthetic public demo fixture: a fictional residential
development with seeded demo records. It does not represent a real submission,
permitting decision, approval, or compliance determination.

## Recommended route sequence

Follow these steps in order through the Brookside Meadows sample project. The
base route is `/projects/proj_brookside_meadows`.

1. Review the sample project. What to notice: the overview links to every part
   of the review-support workflow.
2. Inspect uploaded documents. What to notice: status chips track intake
   handling only; they never imply approval.
3. Open indexed PDF pages. What to notice: open a PDF, then View pages to see
   per-page extraction records.
4. View page-level evidence (evidence search). What to notice: each result
   cites a specific page; results are candidates, not conclusions.
5. Triage evidence candidates. What to notice: candidates require reviewer
   confirmation before they support a finding.
6. Apply checklist review. What to notice: checklist evidence status is
   review-support only; the reviewer decides applicability.
7. Review findings. What to notice: no finding is final without a recorded human
   review action.
8. Record applicant responses (response matrix). What to notice: applicant
   responses are recorded for reviewer review, never as proof.
9. Track resubmittals. What to notice: items are carried forward for review
   across rounds, not closed.
10. Build a reviewer response package. What to notice: a package is a reviewer
    communication record; it does not approve a project.
11. Preview a comment letter draft. What to notice: open a package, then its
    comment letter draft and preview; the boundary statement stays visible.
12. View the reviewer dashboard and workload. What to notice: counts are
    operational indicators only, not engineering outcomes.

## Demo limitations

- This is demo discovery and portfolio presentation, not new backend capability.
- Some deeper records may prompt sign in.
- No full applicant portal, no enterprise single sign-on, no OCR, no live AI
  calls, and no final approval workflow.

## Review-support boundary

Civil Engineer AI supports human plan review. It does not approve plans, certify
compliance, stamp drawings, verify CAD, validate design, declare a project safe,
make final engineering decisions, resolve issues, close issues, or replace a
licensed Professional Engineer. A human reviewer confirms each finding and a
licensed Professional Engineer remains responsible for any final decision.
