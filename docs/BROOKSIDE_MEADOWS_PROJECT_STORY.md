**One-paragraph narrative.** A regional homebuilder, Meadowline Communities LLC,
has proposed Brookside Meadows: a 47-lot single-family subdivision on a 38.5-acre
former farm parcel at the edge of an established neighborhood in the Town of
Hartwell. The site falls from a wooded upland in the northwest toward an
intermittent stream and a wetland buffer along the southeast property line,
where runoff currently sheet-flows to an existing roadside culvert under Quarry
Road. The applicant's civil engineer has submitted a preliminary plan set, a
stormwater management report, a soils report, an erosion & sediment control
plan, and supporting documents proposing a mix of green infrastructure
(bioretention and an infiltration basin) and conventional gray infrastructure
(a wet detention basin and a piped storm drain network). The Town Engineer must
review the package for completeness and technical adequacy before the Planning
Board acts. The package, as submitted, has several gaps and internal
inconsistencies typical of a first submission.

## 2. Site Context (Existing Conditions)

The site is deliberately designed to create realistic review tension across
multiple future modules (stormwater, grading, roadway, utilities, erosion
control, phasing).

- **Existing wooded areas.** Roughly 21 acres of mixed hardwood upland in the
  northern and western portions of the site. Clearing is proposed for roads and
  lots; tree-clearing limits and stabilization timing matter for erosion
  control.
- **Existing meadow / open field.** About 12 acres of old hay meadow in the
  central and southern portions — the former farm field. Lower infiltration in
  places due to a compacted plow pan.
- **Sloping terrain.** The site grades from roughly elevation 412 ft in the
  northwest down to about 358 ft near the southeast corner — a fall of ~54 ft
  across the parcel, with steeper 8–15% slopes on the western wood line.
- **Existing drainage path / stream.** An intermittent stream (locally "Brook
  Run") crosses the southeast corner and conveys site runoff toward the Quarry
  Road culvert. Existing condition is predominantly sheet flow and shallow
  concentrated flow to this corner.
- **Downstream culvert concern.** Runoff leaves the site through an existing
  36-inch reinforced concrete culvert under Quarry Road. Neighbors downstream
  have reported road-edge ponding in large storms. Downstream conveyance
  capacity is a known sensitivity.
- **Possible wetland buffer.** A delineated emergent/forested wetland fringes
  Brook Run along the southeast boundary. The town applies a 100-ft buffer.
  Several proposed grading and outfall features sit near the buffer edge and
  require Conservation Commission attention.
- **Mixed soil conditions.** NRCS mapping and site borings show hydrologic soil
  groups B and C, with a band of slower-draining C/D soils and a possible
  restrictive layer in the lower meadow. Infiltration feasibility varies sharply
  across the site.
- **Seasonal high groundwater concern.** The soils report notes seasonal high
  groundwater within ~2.5–3.5 ft of existing grade in the low southeastern
  portion of the site — directly relevant to whether infiltration and
  bioretention have adequate separation to groundwater.
- **Adjacent residential neighborhood.** An established neighborhood
  ("Quarry Road Estates") abuts the south and east property lines. Abutters are
  sensitive to drainage changes, construction traffic, and tree clearing.

## 3. Proposed Improvements

### 3.1 Roads, Lots, and Pedestrian Network

- **Internal subdivision road.** A single primary collector road ("Brookside
  Drive") entering from Quarry Road, climbing the grade into the site.
- **Cul-de-sac / loop road.** Two residential branches: a loop road serving the
  upland lots and a cul-de-sac ("Meadow Court") serving the lower lots near the
  buffer.
- **Residential lots.** 47 single-family lots, typical 0.4–0.7 acre, with
  proposed impervious cover from rooftops and driveways.
- **Sidewalks / pedestrian paths.** Concrete sidewalk along one side of Brookside
  Drive and a short gravel path connection toward an open-space parcel.

### 3.2 Stormwater Management (the v1 review focus)

A mixed green-and-gray treatment train:

- **Storm drain network.** Curb inlets, catch basins, and reinforced concrete /
  HDPE storm drain pipe conveying runoff from the roads toward the management
  facilities. (Pipe material is one of the planted inconsistencies — see §7.)
- **Wet detention basin (Basin 1 / "Pond A").** A gray-infrastructure detention
  basin in the southeast low area providing peak-flow attenuation before the
  Quarry Road culvert outfall.
- **Infiltration basin (Basin 2).** A green-infrastructure infiltration basin
  proposed in the meadow to reduce runoff volume and provide groundwater
  recharge — feasibility hinges on infiltration testing and groundwater
  separation that are intentionally underdocumented.
- **Bioretention areas / rain gardens.** Two roadside bioretention cells along
  Brookside Drive for water-quality treatment of road runoff.
- **Riprap outlet protection.** Riprap aprons at the detention basin outlet and
  at the culvert headwall to control scour.
- **Naming inconsistency.** The grading plan labels the southeast basin
  "Pond A" while the stormwater report calls it "Basin 1." (Planted issue.)

### 3.3 Utilities

- **Utility extensions.** Public water main extension from Quarry Road,
  gravity sanitary sewer with a single pump station for the low lots, and dry
  utilities (electric/telecom). Utility crossings of the storm drain and the
  buffer are points of future coordination review.

### 3.4 Erosion & Sediment Control and Construction

- **Construction entrance.** A stabilized construction entrance off Quarry Road.
- **Erosion and sediment controls.** Silt fence along the buffer and downhill
  limits of disturbance, inlet protection at catch basins, a temporary sediment
  trap that later converts to the wet detention basin, and stabilization
  measures.
- **Construction phasing.** The narrative proposes two phases (upland first,
  lower meadow second), but the E&SC plan does not clearly tie controls to those
  phases. (Planted issue.)

## 6. Known Design Risks (Realistic Engineering Tensions)

These are legitimate engineering concerns that a real reviewer would track.
They give the project depth beyond the planted document defects:

- **Infiltration feasibility vs. groundwater.** The infiltration basin sits near
  the low meadow where seasonal high groundwater is shallow; separation to
  groundwater is the controlling question.
- **Downstream culvert capacity.** The existing 36-inch Quarry Road culvert and
  reported downstream ponding mean post-development peak flows must not worsen
  downstream conditions — a downstream capacity discussion is expected.
- **Wetland buffer encroachment.** Outfalls, riprap, and grading near the
  100-ft buffer require Conservation Commission coordination.
- **Phased disturbance and stabilization timing.** Clearing 22 acres in phases
  on slopes draining toward a stream raises sediment-control sequencing risk.
- **Maintenance ownership.** Shared green and gray facilities need a clearly
  bound long-term maintenance owner (the future HOA) with access and funding.
- **Green vs. gray trade-off.** The mix of bioretention/infiltration and a wet
  detention basin sets up a future scenario-comparison module (conventional
  detention vs. green infrastructure).

These are planted so the review-support system has concrete, checkable findings.
Each maps to a checklist item and an evaluation case in `SEED_DATA_PLAN.md`.

| # | Planted Issue | Type | Expected Finding |

A well-built v1 should also **avoid false positives** — for example, it should
*not* flag erosion controls as entirely missing (the E&SC plan exists; only its
phasing linkage is weak), and it should *not* claim the project is "approved" or
"compliant" anywhere.

## 8. Why This Story Supports the Whole Platform

Brookside Meadows is intentionally richer than the v1 review needs:

- **Stormwater (v1):** the full treatment train, soils, culvert, and O&M.
- **Grading/earthwork (future):** 54 ft of fall, plow pan, cut/fill phasing.
- **Roadway (future):** collector + loop + cul-de-sac, sidewalks, fire access.
- **Utilities (future):** water/sewer extension, pump station, storm crossings.
- **Erosion control (future):** phased clearing on slopes near a stream.
- **Phasing (future):** two-phase build with sequencing dependencies.
- **Inspection / RFI (future):** seeded inspection note and open RFI.
- **Municipal coordination (future):** Planning Board, Town Engineer,
  Conservation Commission, DPW, Fire.

The same seed project therefore carries every future module without new
storytelling — exactly the "narrow v1, obvious platform" goal from
`PHASE_0_FOUNDATION.md`.
